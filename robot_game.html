Marion Surgical Robot Game


Overview

This game provides medically relevant VR simulations for minimally invasive laparoscopic surgery training. It includes several levels designed to teach the user how to control the tools in a 3d environment using a control scheme similar to that of real medical systems. These levels include general tool movement, camera movement, grasping and dropping hoops onto pegs and suturing training. Also included is a demo of iMSTK's soft body physics within Unreal Engine, surgery on a cyborg's "brain" as well as a prequel demo for the upcoming game Minimally Invasive.

Training Levels

I put together and created each level in accordance to what was asked for by our client, a team a surgeons. The idea was to allow for beginner surgeons to have easy access to a set of controls that emulated the real tools as accurately as possible while being affordable. This included controls such as a clutch system, allowing the freezing of the tool's position in space, allowing the surgeon to move the controller to a different orientation and re-enable control. Additionally, the unique camera control system, which was described as imagining the camera's view as a piece of paper that you grab with both hands and can move around and turn, is a difficult system to get practice for. The camera controls worked by grabbing a snapshot of the position of the controllers when both clutch buttons were held down (the trigger to enable camera movement) and comparing the locations of the controllers to their origin snapshot for panning and comparing the vector between the snapshots and the vector between the two current positions of the controllers to calculate the rotation of the camera. These levels keep track of several metrics that can be used to determine the player's skill with the tools. The metrics include time to complete the procedure, number of collisions of the tools, the total distance covered by the tools for the procedure and the amount of time the tools spent out of view of the camera.

Cyborg Surgery Level

The cyborg level was created to act as an abstraction from the surgical training levels while providing practice for the control of the tools in a more interesting setting. I created a series of puzzles and mini-games using the tools including precision welding, guiding a laser though a maze using mirrors, matching wires of the same color and removing, fabricating and replacing a faulty microchip.

Minimally Invasive Prequel

A short gameplay experience in which you fly a bus around a body cavity and perform surgery to remove tumors infecting the liver of a patient while avoiding antibody enemies attacking your vehicle. For this demo and Minimally Invasive, I developed controls for a new set of tools that are physics-based and have improved fidelity. Since the bus is also mobile, the tools required redesigned movement to allow the player to deploy and control them from any angle that the vehicle is positioned at.
