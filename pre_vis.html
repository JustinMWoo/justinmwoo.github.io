AI Film Previsualization App Demo


Overview

This app uses pixel streaming and AI to allow the user to visualize and create the movie scenes they envision before going out into the field and expending the costs to do it in real life. The app provides a set of tools including, AI mesh generation along side Unreal's lighting and camera settings to provide an accurate representation of the user's vision as well as controls for mobile devices for ease of use. Additionally, the app uses gaussian splats to provide realistic environments and a bride to ComfyUI to turn screenshots from the application into storyboarding images.

Mesh Generation

By providing the url to an image or uploading a png to the pixel streaming website, the application will generate a 3d model using Hunyuan3D. It works by sending the inputs to Synexa Ai's API and pinging the API until the model is finished generating and then prompts a download for it. Then the model is then imported into the engine at runtime using the plugin glTFRuntime. This model can then be spawned in, manipulated, and positioned around the scene and can also be downloaded by the user.

Cameras and Lighting

The user can spawn in both point lights and a directional light to further enhance the scene and manipulate it to how they would like it to look. The lights may be positioned just like the models, however they have additional settings including intensity and color. Cameras can also be spawned in and moved like the lights and models. They will provide a view of the camera in the corner of the screen and the user can also take control of them to move them around the scene more naturally. Cameras also have additional settings related to the lens and LUTs for filters provided through a UI based around Unreal's VCAM.

Gaussian Splats

Gaussian splats are imported into the engine to provide realistic environments for the users to plan scenes. In the future, scans of prominent shooting locations as well as additional scans from user requests will be available as levels in the application.

ComfyUI

After the scene is set to the user's liking, they can press the screenshot button to capture the view. This view will then be displayed in the pixel streaming webpage where inputs can be set for ComfyUI. The image can then be sent to be processed by ComfyUI (running on the same device as the application) in order to create a storyboarding sketch version of the screenshot and many more possibilities in the future with different ComfyUI workflows.
